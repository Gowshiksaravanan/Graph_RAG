Harry Potter Series - Major Events and Wizarding History

The wizarding world has a rich history stretching back over a millennium. The following account covers the most significant events from the founding of Hogwarts through the defeat of Lord Voldemort.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What happened in the First Wizarding War?

The First Wizarding War was a major conflict in the wizarding world that took place during the 1970s. Lord Voldemort, born Tom Marvolo Riddle at Wool's Orphanage in London on December 31, 1926, rose to power by gathering followers known as Death Eaters. Prominent Death Eaters included Lucius Malfoy, Bellatrix Lestrange, Barty Crouch Jr., Severus Snape (who later became a double agent), Peter Pettigrew, and the Lestrange brothers Rabastan and Rodolphus. Voldemort and his followers terrorized both the wizarding and Muggle worlds, seeking to establish pure-blood supremacy and overthrow the Ministry of Magic. The Order of the Phoenix, led by Albus Dumbledore, fought against Voldemort's forces. Many lives were lost during this period, including those of Fabian and Gideon Prewett, Molly Weasley's brothers. The war ended on October 31, 1981, when Voldemort traveled to Godric's Hollow to kill the Potters based on a prophecy made by Sybill Trelawney. After murdering James and Lily Potter, Voldemort attempted to kill infant Harry, but the Killing Curse rebounded due to Lily's sacrificial protection, destroying Voldemort's physical body and leaving Harry with his famous lightning-bolt scar.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What happened during Harry's years at Hogwarts?

In Harry's first year at Hogwarts in 1991, he discovered that Professor Quirinus Quirrell was hosting Voldemort on the back of his head and was attempting to steal the Philosopher's Stone, created by Nicolas Flamel, which could produce the Elixir of Life. Harry, Ron, and Hermione navigated past protections set by the Hogwarts professors, including a giant chess set, Devil's Snare, and enchanted keys. Harry confronted Quirrell in the final chamber and prevented Voldemort from obtaining the Stone.

In the second year, 1992, the Chamber of Secrets was opened by Tom Riddle's diary, which possessed Ginny Weasley. The basilisk within the Chamber petrified several students, including Hermione and Colin Creevey. Harry discovered he was a Parselmouth, could speak to snakes, and ultimately entered the Chamber where he defeated the basilisk with the Sword of Gryffindor and destroyed the diary with a basilisk fang.

In the third year, 1993, Sirius Black escaped from Azkaban prison. Harry learned the truth that Sirius was his godfather and was innocent of betraying his parents. The real traitor was Peter Pettigrew, who had been living disguised as Ron's pet rat Scabbers for twelve years. Professor Remus Lupin, a werewolf and friend of Harry's parents, taught Defence Against the Dark Arts that year. Harry learned to cast the Patronus Charm to fight Dementors.

In the fourth year, 1994, Hogwarts hosted the Triwizard Tournament alongside Beauxbatons Academy and Durmstrang Institute. The champions were Cedric Diggory from Hogwarts, Fleur Delacour from Beauxbatons, Viktor Krum from Durmstrang, and Harry, who was entered by Barty Crouch Jr. disguised as Alastor Moody using Polyjuice Potion. The tournament ended in tragedy when the trophy transported Harry and Cedric to a graveyard in Little Hangleton, where Peter Pettigrew murdered Cedric and used Harry's blood in a ritual to restore Voldemort to a physical body.

In the fifth year, 1995, the Ministry of Magic, led by Cornelius Fudge, refused to acknowledge Voldemort's return. Dolores Umbridge was appointed as Defence Against the Dark Arts teacher and later High Inquisitor of Hogwarts. Harry formed Dumbledore's Army (the DA), a secret student group that met in the Room of Requirement to practice defensive magic. The year culminated in the Battle of the Department of Mysteries at the Ministry of Magic, where Sirius Black was killed by Bellatrix Lestrange, and the prophecy concerning Harry and Voldemort was destroyed.

In the sixth year, 1996, Dumbledore gave Harry private lessons about Voldemort's past using the Pensieve, and they discovered the existence of Horcruxes. Dumbledore and Harry traveled to a seaside cave to retrieve Slytherin's locket, but it turned out to be a fake left by Regulus Arcturus Black (R.A.B.). Upon returning to Hogwarts, they found the castle under attack by Death Eaters who had entered through a Vanishing Cabinet repaired by Draco Malfoy. Severus Snape killed Albus Dumbledore atop the Astronomy Tower, fulfilling a promise he had made to Dumbledore.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What happened during the search for Horcruxes and the final battle?

After Dumbledore's death, Harry, Ron, and Hermione dropped out of Hogwarts to search for and destroy the remaining Horcruxes. They infiltrated the Ministry of Magic to steal Slytherin's locket from Dolores Umbridge. After months of hardship, they learned about the Deathly Hallows from Xenophilius Lovegood: the Elder Wand, the Resurrection Stone, and the Cloak of Invisibility, which together would make one the Master of Death. Harry already possessed the Cloak of Invisibility, inherited from his father James. The trio was captured and brought to Malfoy Manor, where Hermione was tortured by Bellatrix Lestrange. The house-elf Dobby rescued them but was killed by Bellatrix during the escape.

Harry, Ron, and Hermione broke into Gringotts Bank with the help of the goblin Griphook to retrieve Hufflepuff's cup from the Lestrange vault. They escaped on a dragon. They then returned to Hogwarts for the final battle, finding Ravenclaw's diadem in the Room of Requirement. The Battle of Hogwarts was the climactic confrontation between the Order of the Phoenix, Dumbledore's Army, and Hogwarts defenders against Voldemort and his Death Eaters. Many died during the battle, including Fred Weasley, Remus Lupin, Nymphadora Tonks, Colin Creevey, and Severus Snape (killed by Nagini on Voldemort's orders). Neville Longbottom killed Nagini with the Sword of Gryffindor, destroying the final Horcrux. Harry sacrificed himself in the Forbidden Forest but survived because Voldemort had used Harry's blood to regenerate, tethering Harry to life. In the final duel in the Great Hall, Harry defeated Voldemort, whose Killing Curse rebounded due to the Elder Wand's loyalty to Harry.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What happened after the war?

After the Second Wizarding War, the wizarding world entered a period of rebuilding. Kingsley Shacklebolt became Minister for Magic. Harry Potter became an Auror and eventually Head of the Auror Office at the Ministry of Magic. Hermione Granger worked in the Department of Magical Law Enforcement and eventually became Minister for Magic. Ron Weasley worked at Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes with George before becoming an Auror. Harry married Ginny Weasley and they had three children: James Sirius Potter, Albus Severus Potter, and Lily Luna Potter. Ron married Hermione and they had two children: Rose Granger-Weasley and Hugo Granger-Weasley. Neville Longbottom became the Herbology professor at Hogwarts. Draco Malfoy married Astoria Greengrass and had a son, Scorpius Malfoy.
