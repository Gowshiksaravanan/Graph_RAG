Harry Potter Series - Characters and Plot Overview

Harry Potter is the protagonist of the Harry Potter series of novels by J. K. Rowling. The plot of the seven-book series chronicles seven years in the life of the orphan Harry, who, on his eleventh birthday, learns he is a wizard. He attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he receives guidance from the headmaster Albus Dumbledore and becomes friends with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. Harry learns that during his infancy, the dark wizard Lord Voldemort murdered his parents James Potter and Lily Potter but was unable to kill him as well. The plot of the series revolves around Harry's struggle to adapt to the wizarding world and defeat Voldemort.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: Who are the main characters of the Harry Potter series?

The main characters include Harry Potter, the Boy Who Lived and chosen one of the prophecy. His best friends are Ron Weasley, the sixth son of Arthur Weasley and Molly Weasley, and Hermione Granger, a Muggle-born witch known for her intelligence and skill in magic. Other major characters include Albus Dumbledore, the wise and powerful Headmaster of Hogwarts; Severus Snape, the complex Potions Master with a hidden loyalty; Rubeus Hagrid, the half-giant Keeper of Keys and Grounds at Hogwarts who first introduces Harry to the wizarding world; and Lord Voldemort, born Tom Marvolo Riddle, the series' primary antagonist who seeks immortality and pure-blood supremacy.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What is the Weasley family like?

The Weasley family is a large, loving pure-blood wizarding family. Arthur Weasley works at the Ministry of Magic in the Misuse of Muggle Artifacts Office. Molly Weasley is a devoted mother to seven children. The children, in order, are Bill Weasley, who works as a curse-breaker for Gringotts Bank and marries Fleur Delacour; Charlie Weasley, who studies dragons in Romania; Percy Weasley, who works at the Ministry and temporarily becomes estranged from the family; Fred and George Weasley, the mischievous twins who open Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes joke shop in Diagon Alley; Ron Weasley, Harry's best friend; and Ginny Weasley, who eventually becomes Harry's wife. The Weasley family lives at The Burrow near the village of Ottery St Catchpole in Devon, England.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: Tell me about the Hogwarts houses and their founders.

Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry was founded over a thousand years ago by four powerful witches and wizards. Godric Gryffindor valued bravery and courage, and his house's emblem is the lion. Helga Hufflepuff valued hard work, patience, and loyalty, with the badger as her emblem. Rowena Ravenclaw prized intelligence and wit, symbolized by the eagle. Salazar Slytherin valued ambition and cunning, with the serpent as his symbol. Students are sorted into houses by the Sorting Hat at the beginning of their first year. Notable Gryffindors include Harry Potter, Hermione Granger, the Weasley family, Neville Longbottom, and Minerva McGonagall. Notable Slytherins include Draco Malfoy, Severus Snape, and Tom Riddle (Voldemort). The heads of house during Harry's time were Minerva McGonagall for Gryffindor, Severus Snape for Slytherin, Filius Flitwick for Ravenclaw, and Pomona Sprout for Hufflepuff.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What are the major locations in the series?

The primary locations include Hogwarts Castle in Scotland, which houses the Great Hall, the Forbidden Forest, the Room of Requirement, the Chamber of Secrets, and the Quidditch pitch. Diagon Alley in London is the main shopping district for witches and wizards, containing shops like Ollivanders wand shop, Flourish and Blotts bookshop, Gringotts Wizarding Bank, and the Leaky Cauldron pub. Platform Nine and Three-Quarters at King's Cross Station is where students board the Hogwarts Express. Hogsmeade is the only all-wizarding village in Britain, featuring The Three Broomsticks pub, Honeydukes sweet shop, and the Shrieking Shack. The Ministry of Magic is located underground in London. Godric's Hollow is where Harry's parents were murdered and where Dumbledore grew up. Grimmauld Place number twelve serves as the headquarters for the Order of the Phoenix. Azkaban is the wizarding prison, guarded by Dementors. Malfoy Manor serves as Voldemort's headquarters during the Second Wizarding War.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What is the Order of the Phoenix?

The Order of the Phoenix is a secret organization founded by Albus Dumbledore to fight Lord Voldemort and the Death Eaters. Members of the original Order included James Potter, Lily Potter, Sirius Black, Remus Lupin, Peter Pettigrew, Frank Longbottom, Alice Longbottom, Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody, Minerva McGonagall, and others. The Order was reformed after Voldemort's return at the end of the Triwizard Tournament. New members of the reconstituted Order included Nymphadora Tonks, Kingsley Shacklebolt, and eventually many of the younger characters. The Order's headquarters was established at Twelve Grimmauld Place, the ancestral home of the Black family and inherited by Sirius Black. The Order engaged in intelligence gathering, protection missions, and direct combat against Voldemort's forces during both the First and Second Wizarding Wars.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What are Horcruxes?

Horcruxes are objects in which a Dark wizard has hidden a fragment of their soul for the purpose of attaining immortality. To create a Horcrux, one must commit murder, which tears the soul. Lord Voldemort created seven Horcruxes, making him extraordinarily difficult to kill. The seven Horcruxes were: Tom Riddle's diary, which possessed Ginny Weasley in the Chamber of Secrets; Marvolo Gaunt's ring, which contained the Resurrection Stone; Salazar Slytherin's locket, hidden in a cave protected by Inferi; Helga Hufflepuff's cup, stored in the Lestrange vault at Gringotts; Rowena Ravenclaw's diadem, hidden in the Room of Requirement at Hogwarts; Nagini, Voldemort's snake companion; and Harry Potter himself, who became an unintentional Horcrux when Voldemort tried to kill him as a baby. The destruction of all Horcruxes was essential before Voldemort could be defeated permanently. Ron Weasley destroyed the locket, Hermione Granger destroyed Hufflepuff's cup, Vincent Crabbe accidentally destroyed the diadem with Fiendfyre, Neville Longbottom killed Nagini with the Sword of Gryffindor, Dumbledore destroyed the ring, Harry destroyed the diary, and Voldemort himself destroyed the Horcrux within Harry during the Battle of Hogwarts.
