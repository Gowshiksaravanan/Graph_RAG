Harry Potter Series - Magic System, Creatures, and Organizations

The wizarding world features a complex system of magical education, governance, and creatures that exist alongside the non-magical (Muggle) world.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: How does the magical education system work at Hogwarts?

Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry is located in a castle in the Scottish Highlands. Students attend from ages eleven to seventeen, covering seven years of magical education. The school is headed by a Headmaster or Headmistress; during most of Harry's time it was Albus Dumbledore. Core subjects include Transfiguration, taught by Minerva McGonagall; Charms, taught by Filius Flitwick; Potions, taught by Severus Snape and later Horace Slughorn; Defence Against the Dark Arts, which had a different teacher every year due to a curse placed by Voldemort; Herbology, taught by Pomona Sprout; History of Magic, taught by the ghost Professor Binns; Astronomy; and Care of Magical Creatures, taught by Rubeus Hagrid after the retirement of Professor Kettleburn. Elective subjects include Divination, taught by Sybill Trelawney; Arithmancy; Ancient Runes; and Muggle Studies. Students take Ordinary Wizarding Level (O.W.L.) exams at the end of fifth year and Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Tests (N.E.W.T.s) at the end of seventh year.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What is the Ministry of Magic and how is the wizarding world governed?

The Ministry of Magic is the governing body of the wizarding community in Britain. It is located underground in London, accessible through various entrances including a visitor's entrance via a telephone booth. The head of the Ministry is the Minister for Magic. During the series, the Ministers include Cornelius Fudge, who refused to believe Voldemort had returned; Rufus Scrimgeour, who replaced Fudge; Pius Thicknesse, who was controlled by Death Eaters through the Imperius Curse; and Kingsley Shacklebolt, who took over after the war. Major departments include the Department of Magical Law Enforcement, the Department of Mysteries, the Department of Magical Accidents and Catastrophes, the Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures, and the Department of International Magical Cooperation. The Wizengamot serves as the wizarding high court, with Dumbledore serving as Chief Warlock before being removed by Fudge. The Auror Office is the elite law enforcement division that investigates Dark magic.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What are the Deathly Hallows?

The Deathly Hallows are three legendary magical objects supposedly created by Death himself for the three Peverell brothers. The Elder Wand, also known as the Wand of Destiny, is the most powerful wand in existence. It was crafted from elder wood with a Thestral tail hair core. Its known masters include Antioch Peverell, Emeric the Evil, Egbert the Egregious, Godelot, Hereward, Barnabas Deverill, Loxias, Arcus, Livius, Gregorovitch, Gellert Grindelwald, Albus Dumbledore, Draco Malfoy, and finally Harry Potter. The Resurrection Stone allows the holder to bring back shades of the dead. It was passed down through the Gaunt family and was set into Marvolo Gaunt's ring, which Voldemort turned into a Horcrux. Dumbledore found and used it, suffering a fatal curse in the process. Harry used the Stone before sacrificing himself in the Forbidden Forest. The Cloak of Invisibility, unlike ordinary invisibility cloaks, never fades or becomes opaque. It was passed down through the Potter family from Ignotus Peverell and given to Harry by Dumbledore.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What magical creatures appear in the series?

The wizarding world contains numerous magical creatures. Phoenixes are birds that burst into flame when they die and are reborn from the ashes. Fawkes is Dumbledore's phoenix, whose tears have healing powers. Dragons are fearsome creatures; notable breeds include the Hungarian Horntail that Harry faced in the Triwizard Tournament, and the Ukrainian Ironbelly that guarded the deep vaults of Gringotts. Hippogriffs are half-eagle, half-horse creatures; Buckbeak was owned by Hagrid and later by Sirius Black. House-elves are magical servants bound to wizarding families; notable house-elves include Dobby, who served the Malfoy family before being freed by Harry, Kreacher, who served the Black family, and Winky, who served the Crouch family. Centaurs live in the Forbidden Forest; Firenze became a Divination teacher at Hogwarts. Thestrals are skeletal winged horses visible only to those who have witnessed death; they pull the Hogwarts carriages. Dementors are dark creatures that feed on human happiness and guard Azkaban prison. Basilisks are giant serpents whose gaze can kill; one lived in the Chamber of Secrets for a thousand years. Acromantulas are giant intelligent spiders; Aragog was Hagrid's pet Acromantula who lived in the Forbidden Forest.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What are the important magical objects in the series?

Key magical objects include wands, which choose the wizard at Ollivanders wand shop in Diagon Alley. Harry's wand is eleven inches, holly, with a phoenix feather core from Fawkes — the same phoenix that provided the core for Voldemort's wand, creating a twin-core connection. The Marauder's Map was created by Moony (Remus Lupin), Wormtail (Peter Pettigrew), Padfoot (Sirius Black), and Prongs (James Potter), and shows every person's location within Hogwarts. The Sorting Hat, which belonged to Godric Gryffindor, sorts students into houses and can produce the Sword of Gryffindor in times of need. The Pensieve is a stone basin used to review memories, kept in the Headmaster's office. Time-Turners allow limited time travel; Hermione used one in her third year to attend multiple classes. The Mirror of Erised shows the deepest desire of the viewer's heart; Harry saw his parents in it. The Goblet of Fire is an impartial magical judge that selects Triwizard Tournament champions. Broomsticks are used for flying and Quidditch; notable models include the Nimbus 2000, Nimbus 2001, and the Firebolt, which Sirius gave to Harry.

INTERVIEWER QUESTION: What role does Quidditch play in the wizarding world?

Quidditch is the most popular sport in the wizarding world. It is played on broomsticks with four balls: the Quaffle, two Bludgers, and the Golden Snitch. Each team has seven players: three Chasers who score with the Quaffle through hoops, two Beaters who hit Bludgers at opponents, one Keeper who guards the hoops, and one Seeker who catches the Snitch to end the game and earn 150 points. Harry played Seeker for the Gryffindor Quidditch team, becoming the youngest Seeker in a century when he joined in his first year. Oliver Wood was the Gryffindor team captain and Keeper. Other notable Quidditch players include Viktor Krum, who played Seeker for Bulgaria in the Quidditch World Cup, and Ginny Weasley, who later played professionally for the Holyhead Harpies before becoming a Quidditch correspondent for the Daily Prophet.
